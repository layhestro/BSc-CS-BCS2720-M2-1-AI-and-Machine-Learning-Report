The timeline accommodates computational demands of training runs, ensures adequate time for data collection, and allows thorough ML model development and evaluation.

\subsection{Buffer Time and Risk Management}

The timeline incorporates several buffer mechanisms:

\textbf{Parallel execution:} Multiple team members run experiments simultaneously across different environments and algorithms, reducing calendar time despite high computational requirements.

\textbf{Flexible experiment prioritization:} Our tiered experimental matrix (Section 4) allows us to deprioritize lower-priority experiments if time constraints emerge while ensuring high-priority experiments are completed.

\textbf{Phased ML development:} Phase 3 begins ML model development early (Week 11) even while data collection continues (through Week 14), allowing iteration on models as the dataset grows.

\textbf{Extended complex environment timeline:} Complex environment experiments (Walker, Crawler, Worm) are allocated extra time (Weeks 7--9) due to longer training duration (2--4 hours per run).

\textbf{Report writing overlap:} Report writing begins in Week 16 while final experiments and documentation continue, ensuring adequate time for writing, revision, and polishing.
